# LED Message Pillar Brandbook

> A brand strategy and website blueprint for a customizable LED text pillar approximately one foot tall.

## Brandbook Overview

This brand should be built around one unmistakable product: a compact, freestanding LED pillar that turns a name, phrase, logo, number, or memory into an illuminated centerpiece.

The reference website, NeonSigns.com, sells a large variety of illuminated signs. This brand should take the opposite approach: focus on one unique product, make customization simple, and present it as modern personalized decor rather than a traditional sign.

---

## 1. Strategic Direction

### The Big Idea

A small vertical light that turns a name, phrase, logo, number, or memory into a glowing centerpiece.

The product should feel more like a personalized design object than a traditional sign.

### Brand Promise

**We make meaningful words visible, physical, and impossible to ignore.**

### Core Website Goal

Help a shopper imagine their own message on the pillar within the first 10 seconds.

### Positioning Statement

For people who want a personalized object that stands out, the LED Message Pillar is a compact illuminated display that transforms text into modern decor. Unlike a flat neon wall sign, it has a vertical form, a small footprint, and the potential to be seen from multiple angles.

### What to Borrow From NeonSigns.com

- Make customization obvious: text, font, color, size, layout, and optional uploaded artwork.
- Show a proof or preview before production so custom ordering feels safe.
- Use a trust strip near the top of the website.
- Clearly communicate setup, support, production, and delivery expectations.
- Include customer examples so shoppers can quickly understand different use cases.

### What to Do Differently

- Focus the homepage on one hero product instead of a large product catalog.
- Use cleaner, more premium art direction.
- Avoid excessive sale banners and competing calls to action.
- Show the product's scale throughout the site.
- Photograph it on a desk, beside a monitor, held in a hand, on a shelf, and at an event table.

## 2. Product Definition

The product must be understandable at a glance. Confirm the specifications below before publishing final website claims.

### Physical Specifications to Confirm

- Exact height, width, and depth
- Material and finish
- Number of customizable or illuminated faces
- Indoor or outdoor rating
- Weight
- Base design and stability
- Available exterior colors
- Cable and control placement

### Customization Specifications to Confirm

- Character limit per face
- Number of lines available
- Font options
- Text application method
- Logo or image support
- Static-color or RGB lighting
- Brightness settings
- Color-changing modes
- Whether each face can have different content

### Operational Specifications to Confirm

- USB, wall plug, rechargeable battery, or replaceable battery
- Expected battery life
- Charging time
- Production lead time
- Shipping time
- Packaging design
- Product warranty
- Return policy for customized orders
- Replacement policy for damaged orders

> **Important:** Do not publish battery life, waterproofing, color modes, materials, production time, or shipping time until the real product specifications are confirmed.

### One-Sentence Product Description

A compact custom LED pillar that displays your text in a bold vertical format, creating a personalized light for desks, shelves, events, gifts, and branded spaces.

- Birthdays
- Weddings
- Senior nights

- Graduation parties

**Typical uses:**

### Naming Territory 2: Words and Light

**Personality:** Personalized, expressive, and easy to understand.

### Naming Territory 3: Object and Energy

- Pillar Pop
- Beamform
- Lucid Block
- Glowform
- BrightBlock

**Personality:** Playful, product-led, and memorable.

> These are creative directions, not legally cleared business names. Check the domain, social handles, Florida business records, and the United States trademark database before selecting one.

### Recommended Logo System

- Use a bold geometric wordmark.
- Use uppercase or title case with slightly wide letter spacing.
- Avoid script fonts that imitate traditional neon tubing.
- Create a symbol based on a tall rectangle, central light beam, stacked letters, or four illuminated faces.
- Use the symbol alone for the favicon, social profile, product base stamp, and packaging seal.
- Keep the main logo flat and simple.
- Reserve glow effects for product photography, animation, and digital accents.

## 6. Visual Identity

### Visual Direction

**Dark base. Electric accents. Clean product focus.**

The brand should feel premium in daylight and dramatic at night. Bright colors should suggest customizable lighting without making the website difficult to read.

### Core Color Palette

| Color | Hex | Primary Use |
|---|---|---|
| Night | `#090A0F` | Main dark background |
| Cloud | `#F7F8FC` | Main light background |
| Electric Violet | `#7C3AED` | Primary brand accent and buttons |
| Laser Cyan | `#22D3EE` | Secondary accent and lighting effects |
| Hot Pink | `#FF4FD8` | Personal, energetic accent |
| Signal Lime | `#B7F34A` | Small highlights and status accents |

### Suggested Gradients

Use gradients sparingly.

```css
background: linear-gradient(135deg, #7C3AED 0%, #22D3EE 100%);
```

```css
background: linear-gradient(135deg, #FF4FD8 0%, #7C3AED 100%);
```

### Typography

#### Display Font: Space Grotesk

Use for:

- Headlines
- Product names
- Prices
- Buttons
- Short statements
- Navigation labels

Recommended weights: **600, 700**

#### Body Font: Inter

Use for:

- Paragraphs
- Product options
- FAQs
- Forms
- Checkout
- Policy pages

Recommended weights: **400, 500, 600**

### Accessibility Rules

- Neon colors are accent colors, not body-copy colors.
- Use white or near-white text on Night.
- Use Night text on Cloud.
- Reserve gradients for buttons, image glow, borders, and small visual moments.
- Keep important button labels readable without depending on the glow effect.
- Do not use rapidly flashing color animation.

## 7. Photography and Motion

The product is unfamiliar, so every visual should answer a practical question while still making the pillar desirable.

### Hero Image

Show one pillar in a dark, minimal room.

Requirements:

- Text must remain readable.
- Use a controlled light halo.
- Leave empty space for the homepage headline and buttons.
- Keep the background premium and uncluttered.

### Scale Image

Show the roughly one-foot size by placing the product:

- In a person's hand
- Beside a monitor
- On a standard desk
- On a nightstand
- Beside a book or common object

### Detail Images

Show close-ups of:

- Text finish
- Corners and edges
- Product controls
- Cable or charging port
- Base
- Material texture
- Packaging

### Use-Case Images

Create scenes for:

- Gift opening
- Gaming desk
- Bedroom shelf
- Event table
- Reception counter
- Vendor booth

### Customization Images

Show side-by-side versions with different:

- Text
- Fonts
- Layouts
- Light colors
- Exterior finishes
- Numbers or logos

### Motion Direction

- Use a slow 8–12 second product rotation or hand turn.
- Show the vertical shape and any additional customizable faces.
- Animate color changes gently.
- Use a subtle hover glow on website cards.
- A one- or two-degree hover tilt is enough.
- Keep checkout and form pages motion-light.

### Photography Mistakes to Avoid

- Do not photograph the LED so brightly that the text disappears.
- Do not use only pure-black backgrounds.
- Include daylight and real-room scenes.
- Do not use wall-mounted neon signs as the main visual reference.
- Do not hide the cable, controls, or real scale of the product.
- Do not rely entirely on digital renders once physical samples are available.

## 8. Website Architecture

Build the site as a one-product store with a guided purchase path.

### Main Pages

| Page | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Home | Hero, product explanation, use cases, process, customization, gallery, reviews, FAQ, and final CTA |
| Design Yours | Guided customizer for text, font, faces, color, layout, and optional artwork |
| Gallery | Real customer pillars organized by gifts, rooms, events, and business |
| How It Works | Design, preview, production, delivery, and setup process |
| About and Quality | Brand story, materials, construction, packaging, and support |
| FAQ | Lead time, changes, returns, power, controls, character limits, and damaged orders |
| Contact | Support form, order questions, and bulk-order inquiries |
| Cart and Checkout | Clear order summary with all customization choices |

### Recommended Top Navigation

**Logo | Design Yours | Gallery | How It Works | FAQ | Cart**

### Mobile Priority

Most customers may discover the product through social videos. On mobile:

- Keep a **Design Yours** button fixed near the bottom of the screen.
- Show starting price or pricing guidance early.
- Do not begin with a long form.
- Let customers see examples before entering detailed customization information.
- Make product images swipeable.
- Keep the checkout process short.

## 11. Brand Voice

The brand should sound confident, visual, personal, and easy.

It should feel like a design company helping someone create something meaningful, not a factory listing technical features.

### Confident

**Use:** Turn your idea into the centerpiece.

**Avoid:** We think you might really like this product.

### Visual

**Use:** See your words glow from across the room.

**Avoid:** High-quality and amazing.

### Personal

**Use:** What will yours say?

Use **you** and **your** more often than **we** and **our**.

### Simple

**Use:** Type it. Preview it. We make it.

Break technical information into plain-language answers.

### Voice Characteristics

- Modern, not futuristic to the point of confusion
- Premium, not overly formal
- Fun, not childish
- Emotional, not overly sentimental
- Clear, not technical
- Confident, not exaggerated

- Simple line icons
- Rounded corners
- Consistent stroke width

Acceptable:

## 17. Frequently Asked Questions

Replace the placeholder answers once production details are confirmed.

### How tall is the LED pillar?

The pillar is approximately one foot tall. Publish the exact height, width, and depth when the production model is finalized.

### Can I choose any text?

Customers may enter custom text within the product's confirmed character and line limits.

### Can I add a logo?

State whether logos are supported, which file types are accepted, and whether additional design fees apply.

### Will I see the design before it is made?

The recommended process includes a digital preview or proof before production begins.

### How is the pillar powered?

Publish the confirmed power method, included accessories, charging time, and expected battery life.

### Can the light change colors?

Only advertise RGB, color-changing modes, or remote control if the final product includes those features.

### Is it waterproof?

Do not claim outdoor or waterproof use unless the product has a confirmed rating.

### How long does production take?

Provide a realistic production window separately from shipping time.

### Can I return a customized pillar?

Create a clear custom-product return policy covering defects, damage, design approval, and customer-entered spelling errors.

### What happens if my item arrives damaged?

Explain the photo-documentation and replacement process in simple terms.
